Getting Started with SWIM


Take a first splash

First learn about SWIM policies and terms of use.

SWIM uses cookies to manage user credentials and track the selection of modeling outputs. This information is used to measure the platforms’ usage and customize the user experience. Allowing the use of cookies will enable access to the data and modeling features available in SWIM.

Accept the cookie agreement by placing your cursor over the popup notice located at the left-hand corner of the website and press the Allow button. Cookie tracking can be disabled at any time by reopening the agreement box and selecting Deny. The Learn more link will direct you to the SWIM policies.

You can register in SWIM on the Login dropdown (top-right) to stay up to date on new features and privately save your own scenarios, we will skip this step for now.

Swimming into a Canned Scenario

Explore predefined questions of interest, created with input from stakeholders, and see how SWIM can help you answer them. A canned scenario makes a choice of which model to use, what scenario to use, and chooses of what outputs to display based on the relevance to the question. These include river storage and flow, groundwater levels, urban prices, consumption, and climate impacts on crops. You can further customize a scenario, if needed.

Step 1: Access the Canned Scenarios by placing your cursor over the Scenarios dropdown on the main navigation bar and select Canned Scenarios.

Step 2: Start exploring: “Climate effects down the line in a conjunctive water use setting surface and groundwater” by scrolling down to the bottom of the page and clicking on the View Scenario button.

Step 3: Place your cursor over the View Results step, located on the top right of the page. Switch between the summary page and the output catalog by clicking on the dashboard (squares) and book icons to explore the scenario results.

Step 4: View output details by adding a checkmark in the box next to the individual variable name and clicking on the View Selected

For this canned scenario, select the total cumulative loss on the Hueco Bolson and Mesilla Bolson. Note that the climate only directly affects the surface. It can be observed that the loss is much steeper than the recharge on both aquifers. SWIM provides a visual representation of the outcomes of the scenario without requiring additional software. In addition, you can interchange the units used for the visualizations and compare output variables side-by-side.

For more information on browsing model results, consult the Water Balance Model – Output Catalog video on the Tutorials page.
